The Steam Engine

The steam engine was created under the industrial revelution.

It works by heating water in a closed (non-flammable)container vaporizing the water into steam and thus creating pressure.

The steam moves throug a metal pipe that goes to a cylinder with a sliding "piston" the channels the steam into one side at a time

of the piston that spins the wheel(or other mechanisms). This makes the piston move back and forth thus making the wheel spin.

The wheel is connected to a dyno when if its used for power generation. In steam powered trains it is connected to the axel.
